Q1 & Q2 The Two Questions

Two questions. Always answered in sequence. Question 1 establishes the decision chain through First Principles. Question 2 dives into the single hardest problem with STAR-level engineering detail.

Question 1
Tell me your story, the decisions you made along the way, and why you made them.
First Principles decomposition — For each of 2–3 critical forks:
· What was the mainstream approach? (analogical thinking)
· What is the fundamental truth / physical limit / economic essence?
· What counter-intuitive solution emerged?
· Why was it defended despite enormous risk?

Output: A causal decision chain, not a chronological list. Values and motives exposed through decision rationale.
Question 2
Tell me about the hardest problem you've ever solved, and how you solved it.
STAR with extreme detail:
· Situation & Task — Source of "impossibility" in 2–3 sentences
· Action — Failed attempts, hands-on dissection, insight moment, disruptive solution
· Result — Quantifiable: "cost from $1M/kg to $1K/kg" not "significantly improved"

Output: Engineering-level narrative. Personal accountability. Hard numbers.
Output Quality Gate
What the interrogation produces — and what it rejects

The framework is designed to block superficial analysis and force genuine insight. Every output passes through implicit quality gates that distinguish real analysis from description.

✓ First Principles decomposition to physical/economic fundamentals ✓ Causal decision chains — each fork leads logically to the next ✓ Quantifiable outcomes with hard numbers ✓ Honest failure accounting — what was tried, what failed, and why ✗ Descriptive narratives without causal reasoning ✗ Vague adjectives — "significant", "dramatic", "improved" ✗ Third-person observer narration without personal accountability
